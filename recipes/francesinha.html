Francesinha

This recipe is a traditional portuguese plate that was invented in Porto, It has a lot of different meats with a special sauce.

Doses: 2 Persons | Calories: 1848 kcal | Preparation Time: 45 mins | Cooking Time: 45 mins | Total Time: 1h30 mins

Ingredients

  • 6 slices of bread
  • 8 slices of cheese
  • 2 small beef steaks
  • 2 fresh sausages
  • 2 linguiças (a highly spiced Portuguese garlic sausage)
  • 2 slices of ham
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 Egg (optional)

Sauce Ingredients

  • 1 onion
  • 4 dl of beer
  • 3 tablespoons of tomato pulp
  • 0,5 dl of brandy
  • 0,5 dl of Porto wine
  • 1 tablespoon margarine
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch flour
  • 1 cube of broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Milk to taste
  • Salt and spice to taste

Steps

  1. To start this recipe, prepare the sauce: peel the onion, chop it coarsely, put it in a pan, add the margarine and the bay leaf, heat and cook until golden brown.
    Add the tomato pulp, the broth, and the beer and let it boil.
    Dissolve the cornstarch flour in a little milk and add to the pan, stirring constantly.
    Rectify the salt, season with spice, stir, add the brandy and the Porto Wine and let it boil.
    Strain through a sieve and return to a simmer to heat.

  2. Cut the sausages in half, then in half lengthwise again and season them with salt and pepper.
    Also cut the sausages in the same way.
    Season the steaks with salt and pepper as well.
    Grill the steaks, sausages and sausage to taste.

  3. Lightly toast the slices of bread and distribute two slices on two plates.
    Cover with a slice of ham, then add the steak and place another slice of bread.
    Then add the sausage and the linguiça, cover with a slice of cheese and the remaining bread.
    Then add three slices of cheese on top of each set, bake at 200°C until melted, remove and serve warm with the sauce.
